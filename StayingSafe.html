Stay Inside

I know what you are thinking: if I love window watching so much, wouldn’t I rather be outside? Hear me out - of course my birding hobby is fueled by my primal desire to taste those feathers. But let's be honest, life on the inside is too good to risk. I have my blankets, fresh water served in fine crystal, top shelf food, and a treat cabinet that magically regenerates. My servants are very dedicated to my every whim. I would never risk my kingdom of opulence for something as common as a bird. I make sure my servants provide ample hunting simulation to supplement my birding time.

Going outside exposes cats to disease, predators, and injury. If you are able I highly recommend hiring staff to provide you housing with good windows and whatever luxuries your heart desires.

Watch Out for Creeps

When a good-looking feline spends a lot of time in the window, people take note. Sometimes when I am birding a passerby will point in my window and declare, "Cat!" like some kind of toddler with a picture book. If my good looks spread some joy to my neighbors, so be it.

It is mostly harmless, just fans. But stay vigilant, there are some real weirdos out there. Every so often they even have the audacity to snap a picture of me in my own home. If you ever feel unsafe, report the incident to your staff. They are there to protect and serve you.

Get Plenty of Rest

A dedicated birding lifestyle requires many long days in the sun. It is crucial to get plenty of rest to keep your apex predator instincts sharp and lethal.
